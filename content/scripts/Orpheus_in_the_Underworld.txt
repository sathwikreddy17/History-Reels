In the shadowy depths of the Underworld, a haunting melody echoes. But whose song could compel the very guardians of death to listen? 

Orpheus, a gifted musician, once captivated all with his lyre. He fell deeply in love with Eurydice, a beautiful nymph, only to lose her to a serpent's bite. Grief-stricken, he ventured into the realm of the dead, determined to reclaim his beloved. 

As he approached Hades, the god of the Underworld, Orpheus played a sorrowful tune, his heart laid bare. The spirits wept, and even Hades was moved. He granted Orpheus a chance to take Eurydice back, but with one condition: he must not look back until they reached the surface. 

In his longing, doubt crept in. As they neared the exit, Orpheus turned to gaze upon Eurydice, and she vanished forever. 

Love can move mountains, but it is trust that keeps them standing.
