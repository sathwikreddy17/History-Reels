[The sound of rushing water and a deep, resonant voice begins.]

In the heavenly realms, Ganga, the sacred river goddess, shines with divine radiance. Her waters? Pure, life-giving, and full of promise. But all is not well.

[The music shifts to a tense, urgent tone.]

Enter Bhagiratha, a sage with a heavy heart. His ancestors are cursed, trapped in darkness, their souls pleading for peace. Only Ganga's sacred waters can save them.

[Visual of Bhagiratha, weary but resolute, in deep meditation.]

Years of penance have led him here, impressing even Lord Brahma. The gods agree—Ganga must descend. But her power is overwhelming; a direct fall would shatter the world!

[Quick cut to Lord Shiva on Mount Kailash, hair flowing like rivers.]

Shiva steps in, ready to catch her. As Ganga descends, she feels the weight of sacrifice—the glory of heaven fading, her duty to humanity calling.

[Visual of shimmering waters flowing down, soft yet strong.]

When Ganga touches Earth, salvation washes over Bhagiratha’s ancestors. But her gift is eternal—purifying souls, washing away sins.

[The music swells, evoking emotion.]

From that moment, the Ganges becomes a lifeline, a blessing to all. Ganga teaches us: true greatness lies in selfless service. 

[The sound of flowing water fades, leaving a lingering sense of peace.]
